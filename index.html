Turkiye

The climate in Türkiye in detail In Türkiye, it is hot in summer. On the other hand, the climate of Istanbul is not necessarily the same as that of Naples at the same latitude. The Bosphorus, the Black Sea, the Sea of ​​Marmara create a microclimate that defies weather forecasts. Istanbul experiences a hot and humid summer. The morning mists which surround the Bosphorus sometimes follow stormy showers in the evening. On the Aegean Sea and the Mediterranean, same climate as in Greece, even warmer on the south coast, while the north coast, towards the straits, is much wetter. On the plains near Syria, it is scorching in summer. Central Anatolia has its own climate, continental and semi-desert, making it a land extensive breeding. At an altitude of 900 m, this immense plateau benefits from a hot but healthy summer, with cool nights. To the east, the steppe gives way to high pastures, surrounded by mountains. Other havens of freshness, the Taurus Mountains in the south, the foothills of the Caucasus and the Pontic chain North. In winter, the climate on the coast is mild and quite rainy. Istanbul offers a little drizzle this season very refreshing and a few days of snow. Inside, it's cold. In eastern Anatolia, winters are long and harsh: temperatures can drop as low as -30°C and snow can fall from October until May.

When to go to Turkiye

The best times to go to Turkey are spring and autumn, when the climate is most conducive to visit. Between October and April, many sites close, and it can be too hot in summer depending on the region. In addition, summer being the most touristy period, those allergic to crowds could find themselves suffocated. We prefer the months of May to September to visit the North-East of the country: it can be too cold during the rest of the year.

Pakistan

Three seasons divide the year: a cool season between October and February (with dry days and cool nights), a hot season from March to June (hot and humid), and a distinctly humid season from July to September, these seasons leading to fairly variable conditions depending on the region. You will choose the season depending on the place to visit, rather between November and April for the south, and from May to October for the north.

This country in southern Asia extends from the Karakoram and the Himalayas (in the north) to near the Tropic of Cancer (in the south). The relief mainly concerns a large northwest half and culminates at K2 (in the far northeast) at 8,611 meters above sea level (it is the second highest peak in the world, after Everest). With altitude, temperatures decrease and, overall, precipitation increases, with the climate gradually changing accordingly. In the southwestern three-quarters of Pakistan, the climate is generally hot arid (as in Karachi) or hot semi-arid (but cold arid, cold semi-arid or temperate due to the altitude). In the northeastern quarter, the most mountainous area of ​​the country, the climate is arid and cold in the part closest to the Chinese border, temperate (as in Islamabad), continental or alpine, depending on altitude, elsewhere. Pakistan can be exposed to tropical cyclones and tropical depressions from April to December (with, on average and for the entire northern Indian Ocean basin, a peak in May and a second in November). Also note that a “simple” tropical depression can cause more or less significant damage and cause victims… Be careful, sand/dust storms may occur in Pakistan.

Islamabad

the capital, in the north, at more than 500 meters above sea level, there is more than 1,200 millimeters (1.2 meters) of precipitation per year. On average, precipitation is quite low to very moderate in May and from October to December while it is very abundant in July and August. Winters are very mild to extremely mild during the day, quite cold (monthly average of 3°C in January) to very cool at night. Summers are scorching during the day (38 in June), hot at night. Note that during the day the average temperatures are higher in May than in July and August...

Lahore

Lahore's climate is subtropical, with very mild winters (but during which it is often cold at night) and quite dry, and very hot summers, and rainy due to the Indian monsoon. The monsoon spreads approximately from July to mid-September. The city is the capital of Punjab and is located in the northeast of Pakistan, on the banks of the Ravi River (a tributary of the Indus), at an altitude of 220 meters and at 31 degrees north latitude. Beyond the border with India, about 30 km to the east is the city of Amritsar.

Japon

The Japanese climate presents strong contrasts due to the country's spread in latitude (nearly 3,000 kilometers from north to south): we go from a temperate-cold climate in the north (Hokkaido) to a very humid tropical climate in the south (Kyushu and Shikoku). The best times to visit are spring (March to May) and autumn (October and November). April sees cherry trees and azaleas, camellias, wisteria bloom… The colors of autumn blaze at the end of October and the beginning of November. Summer (from June to mid-September) is the wettest season. He does hot, but very humid. Short rainy season (around 3 weeks) in June: precipitation is then almost continuous! Risk of typhoons in August and September. Winter (December to February) is cold and windy, but dry. The sky is generally clear and blue. However, it snows heavily in Hokkaido and on the coast west, facing the Sea of ​​Japan. Average temperatures: - Tokyo and its region: from 14.1°, in April, to 17.6°, in october ; - Kyoto and its region: from 13.9°, in April, to 17.1°, in October; - Fukuoka and its region: 14.6°, in April, at 18.2°, in October; - Sapporo and its region: from 6.4° in April to 10.8° in October.

When to go to Japan

The best times to discover Japan are from the end of March to the beginning of April and from September to November. At spring, the country reveals its superb cherry trees in bloom, while in autumn, its parks and gardens peaceful areas are adorned with flamboyant colors. In addition to admiring magnificent foliage, you can enjoy mild weather, ideal for walks. To contemplate the pale pink hues of cherry blossoms in big cities like Tokyo, Kyoto and Osaka, we advise you to go there at the beginning of April. Remember that while nature can be unpredictable, the milder the climate, the earlier the cherry buds will open in the season. For a stay placed under the sign of Japanese relaxation, visit Buddhist temples and Shinto shrines before you relax in public baths (sentō) or hot springs (onsen). Famous for its delicious cuisine, the Japon will delight your taste buds with its sushi, udon and tofu, between two panoramic walks.

China

Overall, the South is hot and humid, while the North is rather dry and windy, but most of the regions in the '18 provinces of China' lie in the temperate zone. The temperature range from one region to another is significant in winter, but markedly reduces in summer. The arid regions of the Northwest (Inner Mongolia and Xinjiang) experience large temperature variations, with scorching and dry summers, punctuated by a few violent thunderstorms, and cold and very dry winters. The Tibetan Plateau, with an average altitude of 4,000 meters, experiences extremely cold and harsh winters and short, relatively mild summers. It receives abundant sunshine all year round. In the northeast, summers are brief and cool, while winters are long and harsh. Descending into the great North China Plain, where Beijing is located, spring and autumn are quite short but superb. Around mid-April, northwesterly winds carry sands from Mongolia into the plain, known as 'yellow sandstorms.' Along the eastern coastal regions, the climate is oceanic, mild and humid, with well-defined four seasons, while in the loess plains along the Yellow River, summers tend to be longer. In the central agricultural region along the Yangtze River, summers are always hot and very rainy. This is where Wuhan, the first of China's 'five furnaces,' is located. The climate of South China is subtropical, with mild and humid winters, and hot and rainy summers. The extreme southern fringe of Yunnan, Guangxi, and Guangdong, and the island of Hainan enjoy a tropical climate, which explains the abundance of year-round harvests. Special mention for the Yunnan Plateau, which is still the most privileged region: not only does it enjoy pleasant temperatures throughout the year, but it is also home to the greatest variety of animal and plant species. However, it can still snow in winter due to the altitude (around 2,000 meters).

When to go to China

The best seasons to travel to China are autumn (September-October) and spring (late April to early June), periods during which you can wear light clothing, with a small jacket or a sweatshirt. These are also the seasons when the lights are the most beautiful. In Beijing, in July, during the day, the sky is never really blue, it is often veiled by mists of heat and pollution. In summer (from June to August), you must wear very light clothing (except at altitude); in winter (from December to February), warm clothing (no heating in the South, with rare exceptions!), gloves and hats in the North. Waterproof clothing is never too much for the South, whatever the season.

India

A continent country representing a territory as vast as Europe, India has very varied climates depending on regions and altitude. India has three main seasons: winter, summer, and the monsoon season (or rain season). rains). The winter months (November to March) are pleasant throughout India (excluding Himalayan regions): days clear and sunny. In the northern plains, the temperature often drops suddenly. The same At the moment, it is snowing in the Himalayan mountains. In southern India, December and January are cool without ever getting really cold. In the east, showers and snow are common from mid-December to February. The summer months (April to June) are very hot over almost the entire surface of the country, but in the regions of mountains in the north, like Ladakh, the climate remains quite cool. The monsoon period changes depending on the region: - In the south: from June to August in Kerala and from October to November in Tamil Nadu - In the east: from May to mid-October (showers still possible even during winter), We can thus travel all year round in India, depending on the monsoons and the reverse seasons which affect the country. The months of May-June and September-October are periods of climatic transition.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

While English is widely spoken in tourist areas, learning a few basic phrases in the local language can greatly enhance your experience. Additionally, carrying a translation app on your smartphone can be incredibly useful. Many countries also offer affordable SIM cards with data plans, allowing you to use translation services and maps on the go.
To minimize the risk of foodborne illnesses, eat at places that are busy and popular with locals, as high turnover usually means fresher food. Avoid raw or undercooked foods, and be cautious with street food, opting for vendors who appear to have good hygiene practices. Drinking bottled or purified water is also recommended.
Public transportation systems vary across Asia but are generally efficient and affordable. Research the transportation options available in your destination city beforehand. Apps like Google Maps, Citymapper, and local transit apps can help you navigate. In many places, signs and announcements are available in English, and locals are often willing to help if you ask.
Staying in hostels or joining group tours are great ways to meet other travelers. Many cities also have expat communities and social groups that organize meetups. You can use apps like Meetup, Couchsurfing, and local event platforms to find social gatherings, language exchange meetups, and cultural events.
Look for accommodations that have high ratings and positive reviews from other solo travelers. Consider staying in hostels, guesthouses, or boutique hotels that are centrally located, making it easier to explore the area. Safety is paramount, so choose places with good security features and close proximity to public transport and essential services.
Keep your valuables secure and be mindful of your surroundings, especially in crowded places. It's wise to share your travel itinerary with someone back home and check in regularly. Learn about common scams in the areas you plan to visit and always trust your instincts. Having a photocopy of important documents like your passport can be useful in case of emergencies.
